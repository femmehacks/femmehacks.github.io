frequently asked questions

You may be nervous about attending a hackathon, or think that they are just simply not for you. This is NOT the case! Look through our FAQ if you have any qualms, quandries, or predicaments. Email us at hi@femmehacks.io, too.

All are welcome at FemmeHacks.

  • place Where will the hackathon be and when?

    This year the hackathon will take place on February 7th-8th, 2020. It is NOT overnight.

    It will be at the Penn Engineering on the University of Pennsylvania's campus in Philadelphia on Friday, Feb 7th, and the Pennovation Center on Saturday, Feb 8th.

    Penn Engineering Address: 220 S 33rd St, Philadelphia, PA 19104

    Pennovation Center Address: 3401 Grays Ferry Ave, Philadelphia, PA 19146

  • people_icon Who can come?

    Any college or high student student that identifies as a woman or non-binary person is welcome. No experience is required!

  • attach_money Do I have to pay anything?

    Nope! Nada! It's free even to get to/from here with our Lyft codes.

  • build What should I bring?

    A computer, charger, and enthusiasm ... and maybe a bag for all the swag!

  • school Is it ok if I'm not an "experienced" hacker?

    Of course! We have a bunch of mentors to help you out, as well as workshops. Last year we had 1 mentor to every 4 hackers.

  • sentiment_dissatisfied I don't have a team. SOS!!

    We'll have a team formation event on Friday, and a mini one Saturday. Don't let that deter you-- you'll be able to find a team super quickly at these events. Most people are in the same boat. We've had winning teams who have never met before!

  • android Which sponsors have you had in the past?

    All kinds! Big tech companies like Google/Facebook/Microsoft/Intel, fintech orgs like Jane Street and Bloomberg, tech-related companies like The New York Times, startups like Squarespace and Curalate, and apps like Snapchat have come in the past. Our goal is to have variety.

  • announcement Can we talk about diversity?

    Yes!! FemmeHacks values diversity of age, race, economic status, gender expression, size, physical ability, developmental ability, learning styles, nationality, religion, thought, citizenship status, and sexual orientation. We welcome all disciplines and interests, experience levels, and skill sets. We promote respect and do not tolerate racism, sexism, homophobia, or other discriminatory behavior or expression.

    FemmeHacks acknowledges the complicated nature of the word “women,” knowing it may not work for many, and can feel unwelcoming or alienating for some. We use * to specifically and intentionally welcome trans and cis women, as well as nonbinary and gender non-conforming folks :). Equality for all, y'all.
